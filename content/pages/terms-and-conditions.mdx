      **Effective Date: October 15, 2025**


      Welcome to the Pan-African Data Project. This site shares curated
      historical data and visualizations about the people, organizations, and
      events that have shaped Pan-Africanism since the late nineteenth century.
      Our goal is to make this research freely available for teaching, learning,
      and further scholarship.


      ### Use of the Site


      You are welcome to browse, search, and cite materials from The Pan-African
      Data Project for educational and non-commercial purposes. We ask that you
      use the materials responsibly and acknowledge the project and its
      contributors when doing so.


      ### Licensing


      All datasets, metadata, and visualizations on this site are released under
      a Creative Commons Attribution–NonCommercial 4.0 International (CC BY-NC
      4.0) license.


      You are free to share, adapt, and remix our materials for non-commercial
      use, as long as you:


      * Credit The Pan-African Data Project;

      * Include a link to the license; and

      * Indicate if any changes were made.


      Learn more about the license here:
      [https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc/4.0/](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc/4.0/).


      ### Citation


      If you use or reference materials from this project, please cite it as
      follows:


      > Risam, Roopika et al. 2024. The Pan-African Data Project.
      [https://panafricandata.org](https://panafricandata.org).


      Proper citation helps others trace the sources of information and supports
      open, collaborative research.


      ### Intellectual Credit and Attribution


      Unless otherwise noted, all content — including text, data,
      visualizations, and design — was created by The Pan-African Data Project
      team. Please do not republish materials in whole or in part without clear
      attribution.


      ### Accuracy and Updates


      The Pan-African Data Project is an ongoing research effort. While we
      strive for accuracy, the site and its data are works in progress. We
      welcome feedback, corrections, and new contributions that strengthen the
      project’s quality and inclusivity.


      ### External Links


      This site may include links to other digital archives, libraries, or
      scholarly projects. These are provided as resources; we are not
      responsible for their content or policies.


      ### Contact


      If you have questions about reuse, collaboration, or citation, please
      [email Roopika Risam](mailto\:roopika.risam@dartmouth.edu).
